# Rejected hotels

Properties considered for Sky & Swim and ruled out, with the reason. Kept so we
don't re-research the same dead ends — and because a few are traps that
aggregator listicles repeat confidently and wrongly.

**The bar:** a real, wade-in, swimmable rooftop or genuinely elevated pool, with
a view worth naming, at a currently-operating hotel with solid reviews. Rooftop
*bars* don't count. Plunge pools, dipping pools and hot tubs don't count.
Ground-level and courtyard pools don't count. Indoor pools don't count unless
genuinely elevated with a view — and none so far have been.

Last updated 2026-07-18.

**Borderline, excluded:**

- **Pendry Washington DC – The Wharf** — real year-round heated outdoor pool with
  Washington Channel views, but detailed reviews place it on a **second-floor
  river terrace**, not a roof. Fails rule 2. Would also be a second Wharf property.
- **Rosewood Washington, D.C.** (Georgetown) — genuinely rooftop, heated
  year-round, good views, but reviewers describe it as small and closer to "an
  oversized whirlpool than something for laps." Same plunge-pool risk. Would need
  dimensions before reconsidering.
- **Viceroy Washington DC** (Hush Rooftop) — real seasonal rooftop pool but
  consistently called "small," and no source states dimensions or depth.
- **The Ven at Embassy Row** — *qualifies on the pool* (heated, 9th floor,
  swimmable) but scores 8.2 on Booking and ranks #99 of 157 DC hotels.
  Excluded on the reviews criterion, not the pool. Reconsider if scores improve.

## Sourcing notes

Aggregators that produced errors during this research, and should not be trusted
without an operator-site check:

- `trip101`, `hotels-with-rooftop-pools.com`, `hotels-with-rooftop.com` — source
  of the Cambria Houston error; repeatedly conflate plunge pools with swim pools.
- `therooftopguide.com` — states the Four Seasons Philadelphia pool is on the
  60th floor; Four Seasons' own site says 57th.
- `lesboutiquehotels`, `thehotelguru`, `boutiquehotelhub`, `nycplugged`, `dinogo`
  — five sites repeating one 2021 blog post's phrasing about 1 Hotel Brooklyn
  Bridge verbatim. One source wearing five hats.

Anchor on the hotel's own site or Booking.com amenity data. Note also that
TripAdvisor, Yelp, Booking.com, Google Reviews and Reddit all block automated
access (HTTP 403), so review *sentiment* can't be gathered at scale — operator
sites and published first-hand reviews are the reliable base.
